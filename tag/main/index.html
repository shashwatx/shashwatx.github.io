    Vegan keto Meals

    This is the second part of a three-part Vegan Recipe roundup. Today I am sharing 33 recipes for vegan savory dishes, all of them vegan and low-carb. Some are side dishes, but most of them are full meals with a good amount of plant-based protein. I hope you like them! If you want to see the first post of this roundup, check out [12 vegan keto breakfasts](http://maria.recipes/veganBreakfast) Vegan paleo is possible! These [vegan cigofte](https://lowcarb-vegan.net/raw-low-carb-vegan-cigkofte-paleo-friendly/) look amazing! If you like middle east food, try some [moussaka](https://lowcarb-vegan.net/vegan-vegetable-moussaka/). Zodles are a favorite of mine. Check out [these ones](http://ketodietapp.com/Blog/post/2014/07/03/Keto-Vegan-Zoodles) from KetoDietApp with avocado, or you can top your zoodles with this [walnut bolognese](http://www.cocooncooks.com/blog-en/mushroom-walnut-bolognese). For pizza, you can have a [cauliflower crust](http://chocolatecoveredkatie.com/2016/09/05/cauliflower-pizza-crust-vegan/), or use a [flax crust](http://maria.recipes/ketoPizza). Cauliflower can be used to make [tabbouleh](https://asweetlife.org/cauliflower-tabbouleh/) as well as a [potato salad that isn't](https://www.healthfulpursuit.com/recipe/potato-salad-that-isnt/)! If you like shirataki pasta, give a go to this [creamy curry noodle bowl](https://www.healthfulpursuit.com/recipe/creamy-curry-low-carb-noodle-bowl), or try this [pad thai](http://meatfreeketo.com/raw-vegan-low-carb-pad-thai/) (you can substitute the peanut butter for cashew or almond butter). This [vegan loaf](https://www.healthfulpursuit.com/recipe/low-carb-vegan-seed-loaf/) looks spectacular! And for some easier recipes, you can try this [bok choy salad](http://thefitchen.com/2014/03/26/10-minute-healthy-bok-choy-salad-with-creamy-tahini-dressing/) (replacing the honey with stevia) or these [Collard green wraps](http://www.popsugar.com/fitness/Collard-Green-Wraps-Recipe-35676671). Soy can be a great source of protein You can use it to make burgers with [white soy beans](http://maria.recipes/veganBurger) or using [mung sprouts](http://maria.recipes/mungBurgers). You can use soy granules for [lasagna](http://maria.recipes/ketoLasagna), [tacos](http://maria.recipes/tacoFilling) or [cabbage rolls](http://maria.recipes/ketoCabbageRolls). And edamame is great as a [snack with some sesame and soy sauce](http://www.pickledplum.com/healthy-snack-edamame-recipe/), or as part of this [keto lo mein](http://meatfreeketo.com/vegan-keto-lo-mein/). And of course, tofu is a real star for low-carb vegan protein This [kale salad](https://lowcarb-vegan.net/kale-salad-with-baked-tofu-and-a-miso-tahini-dressing/) with tahini dressing looks amazing. [Scrambled tofu](https://lowcarb-vegan.net/my-favorite-tofu-scramble/) is great for a quick, easy fix. If you like indian food, you can try my [tofu tikka](http://maria.recipes/tikkaEng), some [spiralized chowmein](http://maria.recipes/chewmeinEng), this [palak paneer](http://maria.recipes/palakPaneerEng) (which uses tofu instead of cheese), this [healthy Indian Curry](http://www.popsugar.com/fitness/Healthy-Indian-Tofu-Curry-5477629) or some [curry coconut milk soup](http://maria.recipes/currySoup). If you are into thai food, you can try my [thai-style curry](http://maria.recipes/thaiCurry) or this [thai coconut peanut curry](http://vegangela.com/2011/07/19/tofu-with-thai-coconut-peanut-sauce/). And you can try my [soy-free tofu](http://maria.recipes/tofu), made with flax and chickpea flour. And for those of you who can tolerate seitan You can use it to make [steaks](http://www.vegansandra.com/2015/11/perfect-homemade-seitan-steaks.html?m=1) (this is my favorite seitan recipe!), [sausages](http://maria.recipes/seitanSausage) or to make [mock chicken](https://lowcarb-vegan.net/seitan-chicken-pieces/).
